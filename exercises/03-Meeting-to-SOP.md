# Exercise 3 — Meeting Transcript → SOP / Job Aid

- **App:** Word (or OneNote)
- **Time:** 8–12 minutes
- **What you'll do:** Turn a meeting transcript or discussion notes into a clean, step-by-step Standard Operating Procedure.
- **Why it matters:** Cuts SOP drafting time by 60–80% and captures process knowledge while it's fresh.

---

## Steps
1. Open **Word** and the **Copilot** pane.
2. Have a transcript or notes ready — a real Teams transcript (non-sensitive) or the sample below.
3. Paste the prompt with your transcript.
4. Refine, then validate the steps with the people who actually do the work.

## The Prompt (copy & paste)
```
Convert this meeting transcript into a step-by-step SOP for [your process name].

Format the SOP as:
- Title
- Purpose (1-2 sentences)
- Prerequisites/Roles involved
- Steps (numbered, clear, action-oriented)
- Decision points (if/then guidance)
- Common issues & workarounds
- Who to contact for questions

Use professional, clear language. Assume the reader is new to this process.

Here is the transcript:

[PASTE TRANSCRIPT HERE]
```

## Sample Meeting Transcript (Contract Modifications Review)

*Download: [`assets/SAMPLE-Meeting-Transcript.docx`](../assets/SAMPLE-Meeting-Transcript.docx). (Text below if you'd rather paste it.)*

**Tyrone (Contracts):** Let's document our process for contract modifications — it's been ad-hoc. What's the flow?
**Sarah (COR Lead):** When a contractor submits a modification request it comes to me as COR. I do a sanity check — is it in scope, does it align with the PWS — then send it to Miguel in Finance for cost analysis.
**Miguel (Finance):** Once I have the mod and cost impact, I check budget. Under $10K and within budget, I flag it "ready for review." Over $10K or affecting other budget lines, it goes to Tyrone for approval first.
**Tyrone:** Right — anything over $10K needs my signature before Compliance. Below $10K, Sarah and I handle it. Hung, where does Compliance fit?
**Hung (Compliance):** I see every request. Pricing-only changes are quick — about 2 business days. Scope, requirements, or timeline changes need ~5 business days. I also check insurance and regulatory constraints.
**Sarah:** So: COR sanity check → Finance review/budget → if >$10K, Tyrone approval → Compliance review by complexity → recommendation back to me and the contractor.
**Tyrone:** Standard mods done in 2 weeks, complex ones 3 weeks — communicate that upfront. Escalations or disagreements come to me; I decide within 2 business days. We'll also use an intake form so contractors give consistent information.

## Refine it (follow-up prompts)
- *"Add a decision table showing the path based on dollar amount and scope impact."*
- *"Create a RACI matrix (responsible, accountable, consulted, informed)."*
- *"Add an estimated timeline for each step."*
- *"Make a 1-page quick-reference card from this SOP."*
- *"Draft an email template the COR can use to notify the contractor of the process and timeline."*

## Verify before you rely on it
- [ ] Dollar thresholds and approval gates are correct (the $10K rule).
- [ ] Roles and contacts are accurate.
- [ ] Steps are in the right order and nothing was invented.
- [ ] Have someone who runs the process review the draft.
