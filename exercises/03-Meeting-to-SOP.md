# Exercise 3 — Meeting Transcript → SOP / Job Aid

- **App:** Word (Copilot in Word)
- **Workflow:** Turn a meeting transcript into a clean, step-by-step Standard Operating Procedure
- **Material:** **SAMPLE-Meeting-Transcript.docx** (in your OneDrive from pre-work) — a "Contract Modifications Review" discussion
- **Estimated time:** 12 minutes
- **Difficulty:** Core

---

## Objective

Capture process knowledge while it's fresh: turn a recorded discussion into a usable SOP, then sharpen it with a decision table, a RACI, and a quick-reference card. Copilot drafts; you validate the steps with the people who run the process.

## Before you start

- [ ] **SAMPLE-Meeting-Transcript.docx** is in your OneDrive (you uploaded it in pre-work).
- [ ] Word open on a blank document, with the **Copilot** pane available.

## What Copilot is doing here

Copilot drafts from a **file you point it at** — no pasting. To reference the transcript, type a forward slash `/` in the Copilot prompt, start typing its name, and select it. Copilot writes the first draft; you confirm the thresholds and roles are right.

---

## Steps

### Step 1 — Draft the SOP from the transcript
In Copilot, type `/`, start typing **SAMPLE-Meeting-Transcript**, and select it. Then run:
> Note: If the document shows as unaccessible just drag & drop the file into the Prompt box
```text
Convert this meeting transcript into a step-by-step SOP for the contract-modifications review process. Format it as: Title; Purpose (1–2 sentences); Prerequisites / Roles; Steps (numbered, action-oriented); Decision points (if/then); Common issues & workarounds; Who to contact. Clear language; assume the reader is new to the process.
```

### Step 2 — Add a decision table
```text
Add a decision table showing the path based on dollar amount and scope impact (for example, under $10K vs. over $10K, pricing-only vs. scope change), with the approver and target timeline for each path.
```

### Step 3 — Add a RACI and timelines
```text
Add a RACI matrix (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) for the roles in the process, and an estimated timeline for each step.
```

### Step 4 — Make a quick-reference card
```text
Create a one-page quick-reference card version of this SOP — the steps, the $10K decision gate, and the standard vs. complex timelines, at a glance.
```

### Step 5 — Draft the contractor notice
```text
Draft an email template the COR can use to notify a contractor of the modification process and the expected timeline.
```

---

## Deliverable

A complete SOP from the transcript, plus a decision table, a RACI, a one-page quick-reference card, and a contractor-notice email template.

## Verify before you rely on it

- [ ] The dollar thresholds and approval gates are correct (the $10K rule).
- [ ] Roles, owners, and contacts match the transcript — nothing invented.
- [ ] Steps are in the right order and the standard vs. complex timelines are right.
- [ ] Have someone who runs the process review the draft before it's published.

## Key takeaways

- The `/` reference drafts straight from the transcript — no copy-paste, no "[paste here]".
- One transcript becomes an SOP, a decision table, a RACI, and a job aid in minutes.
- Copilot drafts; **the people who run the process confirm it's right**.
