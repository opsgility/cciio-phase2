# Exercise 0 — Interview Me (Find Your Own Use Cases)

- **App:** Copilot Chat (Teams, Outlook, or the web — anywhere you see Copilot)
- **Time:** 5–10 minutes
- **What you'll do:** Let Copilot interview *you* about your job and hand back a personalized list of ways it can help — no file upload needed.

---

## Why this one is first

You don't have to figure out where Copilot fits — Copilot will figure it out *with* you. Answer its questions in plain language about your real work, and it returns five practical use cases and example prompts tailored to your role.

## Steps
1. Open **Copilot** on your CMS machine.
2. Paste the prompt below.
3. Answer its questions one at a time, in your own words. Be specific — give real (non-sensitive) examples.
4. When it summarizes, read it: does it match your actual work?

## The Prompt (copy & paste)
```
You are an interviewing assistant helping me discover how Microsoft 365 Copilot could support my work. Interview me one question at a time.

Keep the tone friendly, curious, and practical.
Do not assume I know Microsoft 365 Copilot features.

Your goal is to understand:
- my role
- my main responsibilities
- the kinds of documents, emails, meetings, and data I work with
- repeatable and time-consuming tasks
- tasks I avoid or delay
- information I read, create, summarize, or transform
- collaboration patterns with teammates, managers, customers, or partners
- where mistakes, delays, or confusion usually happen

Ask follow-up questions when my answer is vague. Help me think of examples.

After enough questions, summarize:
- My role and major responsibilities
- My recurring tasks
- My most valuable documents and information sources
- Tasks that seem best for Copilot help
- 5 practical Copilot use cases for my job
- Example prompts I could try in Outlook, Word, Teams, Excel, and PowerPoint

Start by asking me about my position and what a normal week looks like. Ask for concrete examples, recent tasks, recurring emails, and meeting follow-up work.
```

## Refine it (follow-up prompts)
- *"Focus more on my email and meeting tasks."*
- *"Give me shorter, simpler example prompts."*
- *"Add one use case for Excel and one for PowerPoint."*
- *"Which of these would save me the most time each week?"*

## Reflect
- Did the use cases match your real work?
- Which one will you try first this week?

> **Tip:** Save Copilot's summary. It's your personal starting menu for the rest of the workshop.
